OUR VISION

City / Urban

Egypt produced 310 million tonnes of carbon dioxide in 2016, we aim to collaborate with multiple sectors in order to reduce the emissions. In order to protect all life on land from the effects of pollution and climate change.

Landscape

Climate change is showing an ugly face to Egypt’s thousands of mango farms. And it’s not just the mango farms, hundreds of acres of cultivated soil are lost each year due to poor education and lack of natural fertilizers. Our job is to educate these farmers on how to maintain their farms while making sure they can also provide their own source of natural fertilizers.

Rabies

Despite the considerable under-reporting, the average number of clinically reported cases of human rabies in Egypt is around 50 cases annually, with most cases reported among children 5-14 years of age in rural areas mostly. We aspire to build a strong and impactful platform that will influence our children and spread awareness on how to best deal with such a problem while making sure no animal is hurt in the process.

Who We Are
